1. Go to this website: Google OAuth 2.0 Playground (https://developers.google.com/oauthplayground/)
2. Click the small Gear Icon ⚙️ (Settings) in the top right corner.
3. Check the box that says "Use your own OAuth credentials".
4. Two boxes will appear. Paste your Client ID and Client Secret into those boxes, and close the settings window.
5. On the left side of the screen, scroll down to the bottom where it says "Input your own scopes". In that box, type exactly:
   https://mail.google.com/
6. Click the blue "Authorize APIs" button.
7. This will redirect you to a normal Google Login screen. Choose your @gmail.com account. 
   (Note: Google might warn you the app isn't verified. Click "Advanced" and "Go to [Your App Name]").
8. Click "Allow" to grant permission.
9. You will be redirected back to the Playground website. Now, just click the blue "Exchange authorization code for tokens" button.
10. Boom! A box will appear showing your Refresh Token. Copy that long string of characters!

### Important Restrictions & Things to Know

1. **Test Mode Expiration:** If your Google Cloud app is left in "Testing" mode (which is default), the Refresh Token you just generated will expire in exactly 7 days. Your app will stop sending emails until you generate a new one. 
   - **How to fix:** Go to your Google Cloud Console -> OAuth Consent Screen, and click "Publish App". It doesn't matter if it gets officially verified by Google, "Publishing" it just makes the token last forever.
   
2. **Rate Limits:** Gmail allows you to send about 500 emails per day on a standard free account, or up to 2,000 per day if you have a paid Google Workspace account.

3. **Sending As Yourself:** The emails will officially come from the specific `@gmail.com` account you authorized in Step 7. Since it comes from your real account, your sent emails will actually show up in the "Sent" folder of your personal Gmail!

4. **Security Risk:** If your `CLIENT_SECRET` and `REFRESH_TOKEN` are leaked, anyone can read your emails or send emails from your personal account. Always store them securely using Cloudflare Secrets!
